About

As a society, we have perceived progress as constant forward and upward growth, yet our current path has pushed us beyond several planetary boundaries, risking irreversible ecological damage. It is imperative that economies must no longer pursue boundless growth but rather aim to thrive within a safe and just space that enables balance between human needs and planetary health, giving way to the concept of doughnut economics, developed by Kate Raworth.

Inspired by this shift in thinking, I am currently pursuing an MSc in Sustainability Transformation at ESSEC Business School, actively building expertise and experience in the field of sustainability and ESG. Highly organized, adaptable, and resilient, I possess hands-on experience in sustainability services, with exposure to impact and materiality frameworks, benchmarking, and ESG rating methodologies.

Motivated by a desire to deepen my analytical and sector-specific knowledge, I am seeking entry-level roles in sustainability or ESG where I can contribute to impactful projects, advance my technical skills and collaborate across sectors. I am particularly interested in opportunities where I can gain exposure to:

  • ESG consulting or analysis
  • Sustainability reporting and disclosure
  • ESG finance
  • Climate-focused asset management and responsible investment regulation
  • Policy engagement or systems mapping

Proficient in key data analysis tools such as Bloomberg Terminal and S&P Capital IQ for ESG and financial data analysis. Experienced with statistical software such as SPSS and Stata, and programming languages R and SQL, used for large-scale data processing, quantitative modeling, and scenario analysis.

I welcome connections from professionals engaged in climate and sustainability initiatives or those interested in developing a values-driven team.
